Bump Nesting Overview

Although Nesting is a powerful tool that creates a high part to sheet yield, there may be times when you want to manually edit your nested results. Bump nesting gives you the ability to move and copy parts in an existing nest while maintaining the "part-to-part distance” setting. With bump nesting, you can also delete or lock in place selected parts from the sheet.

Placing parts

  • The Drag button returns you to the main graphics window. From here, you can move parts to any open area on any sheet. You can move or copy your selected parts depending on the mode that you set in the Bump nesting ribbon bar.

  • Click and drag the part to place it on a sheet. A grey "guide arrow" displays as the part approaches the edge of the sheet or another part's boundary. Hold the left mouse button to display a red "fine tuning" guide arrow. If your part is irregularly shaped and needs to "snap" to multiple boundaries, you can use this fine tuning arrow to adjust the part's placement.

  • Select the part, and use the [+] and [-] keys to rotate the part by the step angle specified in the Nesting dialog box. Hold down the [Ctrl] key while using the [+] and [-] keys to rotate the part in one degree increments.
  • Select the part and click Mirror to flip it on it's Y axis.

To place a part on a different sheet

  1. Select the part.
  2. Select a destination sheet from the drop-down list on the Bump Nesting dialog box.
  3. Place the part in any open area on the destination sheet.

Deleting parts

After you review your nesting results, you may decide that it is necessary to remove several parts to make room for others. Also, it may be useful to delete parts in problem areas of the sheet. Bump nesting allows you to select and delete specific parts from the sheet.

Click Delete in either the Nesting Results dialog box, the Customize dialog box, or the Bump nesting dialog box. Select the part in the graphics window and press the [Esc] key to complete the process.

Locking parts

Click Customize in the Nesting Results dialog box to access the Lock function. To lock a part:

  1. Click Lock at the bottom of the Customize dialog box.
  2. Select the part in the graphics window. The part color toggles from purple (unlocked) to grey (locked).
  3. Press the [Esc] key to complete the process and return to the Customize dialog box.

If desired, edit the fields on the Sheets and Parts tabs. Locked parts remain in their locations even after you re-nest the sheet using different parameters and part quantities.

NOTE
  • In the Customize dialog box, the Lock and Delete buttons are available no matter which tab is active.
  • Clicking Drag to return to the graphics window releases all previously locked parts. A released part can be moved or deleted.
  • Locking is not available with toolpath nesting.

Access bump nesting features by clicking the Delete, Drag, and Customize buttons located in the Nesting Results dialog box. If you are nesting toolpaths (Toolpaths, Utilities, Nesting), your Nesting Results dialog box displays different options, and does not include the Customize button.
