Art Manager Overview

The Art Manager is a central location where you manage the elements of the current Art model. If Mastercam Art is installed, the Art Manager tab is located along with the Toolpaths Manager and the Solids Manager tabs, as shown below. The Art Manager can be toggled on and off on the View tab.

The Art Manager history tree is a hierarchical representation of the Art base surface and surface operation elements that make up your Art model. It lists all Art base surfaces in the Art model and all Art operations with their current status (clean, dirty, deleted). A red check mark indicates that the Art model, Art base surface, and one or more of its Art operations are "dirty." This occurs when one or more of the following conditions are true:

  • Parameters for an Art base surface or Art surface operation were modified.
  • The underlying geometry for an Art surface operation was changed.
  • An Art base surface or Art surface operation was deleted (represented by an X).

Use the Art Manager's Regenerate function to rebuild the Art model based on the current parameters and settings, and to restore its status to “clean.” Regeneration removes Art base surfaces and Art surface operations marked for deletion from the Art Manager history tree. Clean operations are unmarked. If the entire Art model is clean, the Regenerate function is inactive.

The information at the bottom of the Art Manager displays the following:

  • The current and maximum number of triangles as determined by the Art base surfaces resolution and shading quality.
  • The surface height and if a Z limit constraint is applied, the remaining height before maximum of the Art base surface.

Click the links below to view a description of each element in the Art Manager history tree.

Art model

Represents the highest level of the Art Manager hierarchy. Each Art model can include one or more Art base surfaces and, for each Art base surface, one or more Art surface operations. It also includes an historical record of all events that have occurred since the model was first created.

Use Art Manager functions and right-click menu options to change the Art model display, regenerate it, change its graphics quality, or redefine its resolution.

You can also use the Undo / Redo functions to repeat or undo selected events.

Art base surface (ABS)

A 2D or 3D mesh made up of optimized triangles that defines the extents of a single Art surface area. Large triangles are used in flat areas and small triangles in areas with high curvature. The first step in creating an Art model is to define a new Art base surface. Once that is created, you can use Art surface operations to manipulate this single surface.

Each Art model can contain one or more Art base surfaces. To work with an Art base surface and its operations, you must make it active by selecting it in the Art Manager history tree.

Initially, the Art base surfaces you create using the New Art Base Surface, Rectangular and the New Art Base Surface, Unwrapped Cylinder functions are flat. Those created using the New Art Base Surface, From Image function have dimension.

Art Surface operation (ASO)

Art surface operations allow you to manipulate an Art base surface by adding surface features, often using geometry to build the Art model. You can create 3D elements from 2D geometry and import designs from external files or from the Classic Art library. You can also create textures, and modify or transform existing Art surface operations. Many Art surface operations require you to define the operation's boundaries by chaining geometry in the graphics window. Mastercam Art's nested boundary recognition lets you create multiple complex surfaces in a single Art surface operation, using simple chaining techniques.

To perform an Art surface operation, you must first create an Art base surface. All Art surface operations appear indented under their "parent" Art base surface in the Art Manager history tree. For each Art surface operation in the list, you can view and modify its parameters. You can also, if applicable, re-select the geometry used to create the operation.

NOTE

If you do not have Mastercam Art installed, you can still import, view, and machine Art models. However, you must have a licensed copy of Mastercam Art installed to modify or create Art surfaces and operations.
