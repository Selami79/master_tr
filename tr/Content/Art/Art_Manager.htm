Art Manager

Art Manager functions help you control what is displayed in the graphics window, and are a quick way to make Art model and surface modifications.

Use these controls to manage the following elements in Mastercam Art.

Graphics window
  • Modify the Art model display in the graphics window.
  • Change the degree of Art model transparency in the graphics window.
Art model
  • Analyze points and view statistics on the Art model features.
  • Change the resolution (number of points) in the Art model.
  • Regenerate and rebuild the Art model.
  • Unload or load the Art model from the Mastercam database.
  • View, edit, and delete Art model components in the Art Manager history tree.
Art base surface/Art surface operations
  • Change the sharpness and intensity of materials reflected in Art surfaces.
  • Open the Art Shading Settings dialog box.
  • Set the Automatic Sorting threshold or manually sort the triangles that make up the Art surface.
  • Undo or redo one or a series of Art operations.
  • Move an Art operation up or down the Art manager history tree.
Parameters

Hide geometry

Toggles the display of geometry in the graphics window on and off.

Select to hide all geometry, providing a clear view of the Art model. Deselect to show the geometry.

Hide art model

Toggles the display of the Art model in the graphics window on and off.

Select to hide the Art model, allowing you to better view its underlying geometry and other features. Deselect to show the Art model.

Toggle opacity

Turns the Art model's transparency on or off in the graphics window.

To better visualize Art model surfaces, select this control to view them with maximum (100%) opacity. Deselect the control to view the Art model with the amount of opacity defined in the Art Shading Settings dialog box or in the Art Manager field setting.

Toggle reflectivity

Turns the Art model's reflectivity on or off in the graphics window.

To better visualize Art model surfaces, select this control to view the Art model without the reflection image. Deselect the control to view the Art model with the reflection image at the level of reflectivity defined in the Art Shading Settings dialog box or in the Art Manager field setting.

Art model single viewport

Available only when you set the graphics window display to multiple viewports. Toggles the display of the Art model on and off in a single viewport. In all other viewports, only geometry displays.

You can also use the Art Manager Change Art Model Viewport, Hide Geometry, and Hide Art Model functions to modify the display.

Change art model viewport

Available only when you choose to set the graphics window display to multiple viewports, and also choose the Art Model Single Viewport functions.

Use the Change Art Model Viewport function to cycle the Art model display between viewports.

Analyze

Displays XYZ surface coordinates for any point position you select with the cursor in the graphics window. To exit this function, press [Esc] or choose another function.

NOTE

For accurate statistics, set the graphics view to Top.

Statistics

Displays the Art base surface statistics dialog box where you can view read-only information on the extents of the active Art base surface, including its dimensions, coordinates, and scale.

Art model resolution

Define the number of points per square inch or millimeters in your Art model. Mastercam Art selects a default resolution based on the size of the Art base surface (ABS).

A value of 200 defines the Art model as 200x200, or 40,000 points per square inch / millimeter. Increase resolution to improve the definition and display of the shaded Art model.

The resolution of the Art model is dynamic, which means you can change it at any time. Reducing the resolution can significantly increase your productivity. A lower resolution decreases the file size, so the Art model regenerates faster. Resolution becomes very significant when you have many or complex Art surface operations and Art base surfaces in an Art model.

Art model opacity

Enter the degree of opacity (1-100) displayed by your shaded Art model in the graphics window. The default is totally opaque (100).

The opacity of the Art model is dynamic. It updates the model instantly, and you can change it at any time. Increase opacity to improve the definition and display of the shaded Art model. Decrease it to more easily select the Art model's underlying geometry.

NOTE

You can also change this setting in the Art Shading Settings dialog box.

Art model reflectivity

Set the amount of the light (1-100) reflected by your shaded Art base surface in the graphics window.

Reflectivity is a dynamic setting. It updates the model instantly, and you can change it at any time. Increasing reflectivity creates surfaces that will more exactly mirror the shape and color of the selected reflection bitmap. The shape and color of the reflected image is less distinguishable in an art surface with less reflectivity.

NOTE

You can also change this setting in the Art Shading Settings dialog box.

Art model shading

Opens the Art Shading Setting dialog box where you can define the color, opacity, and reflection quality (material and power) of the Art base surface. Additionally, you can use three methods to crop the Art base surface.

Sort now

Sometimes, especially with non-opaque surfaces or surfaces that have been dynamically rotated, Mastercam Art must sort the triangles that make up the Art base surface to maintain graphic quality. Redraws the triangles that make up the Art base surface so that they are aligned in the proper direction.

Use this function when your Art base surface has exceeded the Automatic Sorting threshold set in the Surface Display Options dialog box.

Surface display options

Opens the Surface Display Options dialog box where you can maintain the graphic quality of the Art model.

Unload / Load art model

Acts as a toggle, allowing you to remove / restore the Art model in the Mastercam part database.

Unloading the Art model removes all data associated with it from the Mastercam part database, and deactivates the Art Manager and most functions in the Art tab. This can reduce the amount of time and resources required to work with other aspects of the part that are not Art related, and can also clear the display so that you can more easily create other types of toolpaths or designs.

After you have unloaded the Art model, select this function to add the Art data back to the Mastercam part database.

NOTE

If you unload an Art model, and then save and close the Mastercam part file, when you next open the file, the Art model remains unloaded. Click Unload / Load Art Model to load the Art model and activate all Art Manager and Art tab functions.

Regenerate

Available only if the Art model needs to be rebuilt to reflect changes.

Regenerates all changed Art base surfaces and Art surface operations in the Art model using the current parameters and completely rebuilds the Art model. You must regenerate elements that are marked "dirty" and "deleted" to see the resulting changes in the Art model.

You can make multiple changes to Art base surface and Art surface parameters / geometry and regenerate the Art model all at once to save time.

Undo / Redo

Mastercam Art saves a history of every event that has been processed in the Art model since it was first created.

Use the Undo and Redo drop-down lists to view a list of all actions performed up to this point in time, with the most recent changes appearing at the top of the list.

Highlight the action to be undone /redone and press [Enter] to undo/redo everything above it in the list and returning the Art model to the selected state.

Move ASO up/ Move ASO down

The Up and Down arrows in the Art Manager toolbar change the order of Art surface operations in the history tree.

Select an operation, then click the up or down arrow to move it. Each click moves the operation one level. Click the arrow until the surface is in the desired position. Mastercam Art rebuilds the Art model according to the revised history tree.

NOTE

Use this feature to:

  • Arrange surfaces that should be generated in a specific order.
  • Visually organize large projects by grouping like operations.
