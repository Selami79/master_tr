Solid Position panel

Use the Solid Position function to move solids into contact with other solids—for example, easily position a part onto a fixture. To do this, first select the body to move and then select the contact bodies (the solids onto which you want to position the body to move). Then when you move the body, it automatically highlights and stops when it comes into contact with any of the contact bodies.

In the following illustration, the picture on the left shows a machining table and a part in Mastercam's graphics window. The picture on the right shows the part being moved into contact with the table and the two pins.

Note that Solid Position differs from Align to Face in that the latter aligns only faces with faces.

Using the Solid Position function

The following procedure shows how to use Solid Position.

  1. Select Solid Position on the Model Prep tab.

  2. Select a face on the body to be moved. Mastercam shows the dynamic gnomon. Where the gnomon appears depends on the face you select.

  3. Position the gnomon at the body's center of rotation, and press [Enter]. Mastercam removes the selected body from the screen to give you an unimpeded view of the remaining geometry.

    NOTE
    If you do not need to rotate the body, just press [Enter] when asked to set the center of rotation. You might also want to turn off the Rotate body option in the Solid Position panel. Doing this locks the body into its current orientation. You cannot, however, change the Rotate body option until you have finished the solid selections required by Solid Position.

  4. Select the contact solids (the solids onto which you want to position the body to move), and press [Enter].

  5. Use the dynamic gnomon to position the body to move onto the contact solids, and press [Enter].

Using vectors with Solid Position

In this discussion, the body to move will be called the part. Two options in the Solid Position panel—Vector and Rotate body—allow you to more easily position a solid part. The Vector option aligns the X axis of the part along the selected vector. (Additional geometry defined in the Additional Geometry list also aligns along the selected vector.) Following is an example of using Vector to align the part with a feature on the machining table.

  1. Set up Solid Position as described in Steps 1 to 4 in the previous section, "Using the Solid Position function." Notice in the following image that the X axis aligns with the notch in the part.


  2. Click the arrow button to the right of the Vector option. Mastercam hides the part and returns you to the graphics window to choose a vector.

  3. Choose the first point of the vector.

  4. Choose the end point of the vector. In this case, the ending point is the top of the lower red pin.

    5. Mastercam rotates the part, aligning its X axis with the selected vector. In this case, the notch on the part is now facing the lower red pin.

Using rotation and Solid Position to center a feature on contact bodies

As in the previous section, in this discussion, the body to move will be called the part. The Rotate body option allows you to rotate the part, along with additional geometry, around the center of rotation you defined. (To rotate the part, the Rotate body option must be selected in the Solid Position panel.) You can then rotate the part using the curved arm on the dynamic gnomon.

The following example rotates the part 90 degrees and centers the part's notch between the two pins of the machining table.

  1. Set up Solid Position as described in Steps 1 to 4 in this topic's first section, "Using the Solid Position function." In the following image, notice the orientation of the part's notch.

  1. Click the curved section of the dynamic gnomon, and rotate the part -90 degrees.

  2. Click the Y axis arrow on the dynamic gnomon.

  3. Hover the mouse over the first pin until a temporary point appears, and then hover over the second pin until a temporary point appears there, as well. Mastercam creates a temporary center point between the two pins, as indicated by the red plus mark in the following image.

  4. Click the temporary center point to center the part between the two pins, and use the dynamic gnomon to move the part into position, as shown in the following image.

For more information, hover your mouse over the controls in the Solid Position panel. Tooltips appear, explaining what the options do.
