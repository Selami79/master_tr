Down Cutting Parameters

Use the Down Cutting Parameters dialog box to detect and finish cut regions of a profile with the optimum toolpath using a single chain. Walls—referred to as steep, down cut, and faces—are cut in a vertical or downward direction. Flats—referred to as shallow, non-down cut, and diameters—are cut in a horizontal or upward direction. The specified transition angle and finish direction define what is considered a wall or flat:

  • OD, ID: Walls 90 or 270 degrees are considered vertical
  • Face, Back: Walls 180 or 360 degrees are considered horizontal
Parameters

Strategy

Geometry is processed in the order it is specified in the chain. Choose from one of the following cutting strategies:

  • Walls: Down cuts walls using only a down cut strategy.
  • Flats: Machines flat areas only in the direction of chain.
  • Alternate: Processes all geometry, switching between walls using the wall strategy, and flats using the flat strategy.
  • Walls then flats: Machines all walls using the wall strategy, followed by all flats using the flat strategy.
  • Flats then walls: Machines all flats, followed by all walls.

The benefit of the Walls then flats and Flats then walls strategies are that they require only a single change in direction and cutter compensation.

Reverse cut direction

Flips the direction of the toolpath from the default. The toolpath cuts in the opposite direction, starting from the opposite end of the geometry.

Transition angle

Determines which regions are considered walls or flats. The transition angle is the deviation from the wall angle before the geometry is considered to switch from a wall to a flat. A zero degree transition angle implies that all corner chamfers and radii are part of the wall. A 90 degree transition angle implies that all corner chamfers and radii are part of the flat. When processing arcs, the position on the arc where the geometry transitions between wall and face is calculated and applied accordingly.

Standoff distance

Enter a distance, measured in the direction of the cut, to determine where the tool stops before retracting at the end of the pass. The distance is measured back from the transition point as shown below.

Standoff can be specified as an absolute distance or as a percentage of the tool nose diameter. This prevents the tool from being buried in the stock at the end of the pass. The pass down the wall will be extended by the amount that the pass along the flat is shortened, so the two passes overlap at the same point.

From stock

Select to stop the tool at the defined distance from the stock on the wall. Deselect to stop the tool at the defined distance from the end of the floor. If no stock is defined, this option is disabled.

Overlap

Sets the overlap amount for the wall and flat cuts. Enter an absolute distance or as a % of radius of the tool nose.

Minimum step

Sets how high the wall must be to be considered a wall instead of part of a flat. This can prevent unnecessary down cutting on very short steps.

Extend passes

Extends the start of each pass along the wall and flat. This is useful for starting the pass off of the part. The pass will only be extended if it starts with a linear move. Arc moves are not extended.

Do final pass along entire chain

Adds an extra pass after all of the passes along the walls and flats. The extra pass cuts in a single direction along the entire chain.

Cut depth for final pass X, Cut depth for final pass Y

Sets how much stock is left in X for the final pass after all of the passes along the walls and flats. This is separate from the stock to leave on the Finish parameters tab. For example, if the Stock to leave in X on the Finish parameters tab is 0.25 and the Cut depth for final pass X in the Down Cutting Parameters dialog box is 0.1 then there will be 0.35 stock left after all of the passes along the walls and flats. The final pass will then cut 0.1, leaving 0.25 stock on the part.

If you are using a Y-axis tool, the value of this field is a Y-axis coordinate, not X. When this happens, the name of this field references the Y axis instead of X.

Cut depth for final pass Z

Sets how much stock is left in Z for the final pass after all of the passes along the walls and flats. This is separate from the stock to leave on the Finish parameters tab. For example, if the Stock to leave in Z on the Finish parameters tab is 0.25 and the Cut depth for final pass Z in the Down Cutting Parameters dialog box is 0.1 then there will be 0.35 stock left after all of the passes along the wall and floor. The final pass will then cut 0.1, leaving 0.25 stock on the part.

Reverse cut direction

Determines whether the final pass is in the chain direction or opposite of the chain direction.
