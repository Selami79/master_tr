Cut Parameters page: Saw

Use the Cut Parameters page to configure the Saw toolpath's cutting passes. Follow this general outline:

  1. Enter values for different cutting parameters and compensation options.
  2. Use the pages nested below the Cut parameters page to activate and configure advanced toolpath features.

Compensation type

Choose how you want to handle cutter compensation:

  • Computer: Computes the compensated toolpath and does not output control codes for compensation.
  • Control: Outputs control codes for compensation and does not compute the compensated toolpath.
  • Wear: Computes the compensated toolpath and outputs control codes for compensation. Compensation direction in the computer and control are the same.
  • Reverse wear: Computes the compensated toolpath and outputs control codes for compensation. Compensation direction in the computer and control are opposite.
  • Off: Programs the tool tip directly on the chained geometry and does not output compensation codes.

The active control definition determines the available options.

Compensation direction

Sets the machining direction for the toolpath. Choose one of the following options:

  • Climb: Cuts in one direction with the tool rotating in the opposite direction of the tool motion.
  • Conventional: Cuts in one direction with the tool rotating in the same direction as the tool motion.

The Compensation direction controls the arbor side of the saw relative to the spindle direction that is defined for the tool in the Tool page. The Spindle direction is set as if you are looking at the saw blade from the aggregate side down the arbor. For example, if the spindle rotation for the saw is CW (looking down the arbor), and you set the Compensation direction to Climb, the arbor side of the resulting toolpath is to the right. Use the following chart as a guideline for correctly setting these parameters for Saw toolpaths.

Compensation Direction Spindle Rotation Arbor Side
Climb CW Right
Conventional CW Left
Climb CCW Left
Conventional CCW Right

Tip compensation

Sets amount to offset from the tool's center or tip. Choose Center to have the offset calculated from the center of the saw tool. Choose Tip to have the offset calculated from the tips of the teeth of the saw tool. Used for touching the tool off the part.

Stock to leave on walls

Sets the amount of stock on selected vertical machining geometry. Enter a positive value if you do not want to remove all stock or enter a negative value to remove more stock. If the machining geometry (or a portion of it) is not vertical, Mastercam interpolates a value between the Stock to leave on floors and Stock to leave on walls amounts.

Stock to leave on floors

Sets the amount of stock on selected horizontal machining geometry. Enter a positive value if you do not want to remove all stock or enter a negative value to remove more stock. If the machining geometry (or a portion of it) is not horizontal, Mastercam interpolates a value between the Stock to leave on floors and Stock to leave on walls amounts.
