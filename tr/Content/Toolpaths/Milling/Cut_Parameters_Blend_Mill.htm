Cut Parameters page: Blend Mill

Use the Cut Parameters page to control the cutting motion Mastercam uses between the two open chains you selected for the 2D High Speed Blend Mill toolpath. The blend cutting method can be Zigzag, One way, Spiral, From center, or To center. The Along or Across options let you create cuts that start with the first blend chain and then morph toward the second blend chain in either an across or along direction. For along cuts, you can change the resolution of the tool stepover. Extending your entry or exit motion allows for fine tuning of the toolpath.

If your part has a consistent shape in the region between the blend curves or if the blend curves are similar in shape, you may be able to make the resolution less refined: use a larger distance or percentage of stepover, 500% or 1000%, for example. A larger stepover reduces the toolpath calculation time.

If your part has many small features (bosses or cavities), then you might need to make the resolution more refined: use a smaller distance or percentage of stepover, 50% or 25%, for example. A smaller stepover increases the toolpath calculation time but the toolpath will maintain a consistent stepover.

Cutting method

Orients the cutting direction. Select one of the following options:

  • Zigzag: Cuts each pass in the opposite direction to the previous pass. A short linking motion connects the two ends.
  • One Way: Cuts one direction, steps over to the next cut while making a rapid move to same start side of the cut, and cuts in the same direction as the previous pass.
  • Spiral: Cuts in a continuous motion from one boundary to the other.
  • From Center: Cuts one direction, steps over to the next cut, and cuts in the opposite direction starting from the center and working its way outside while maintaining a climb cut.
  • To Center: Cuts one direction, steps over to the next cut, and cuts in the opposite direction starting from the outside and working its way to the center while maintaining a climb cut.

Compensation direction

Offsets to the tool based on the selected direction. Choose one of the following:

  • Off: The tool center is allowed to travel to the selected chains.
  • Left: The tool is allowed to travel to the left of the selected chains.
  • Right: The tool is allowed to travel to the right of the selected chains.
  • Inside: The tool stays between the selected chains.
  • Outside: The tool is allowed to travel to the outside of the selected chains.

Tip compensation

Select whether the compensation point will be the center of the tool or the tip. Compensating to the center of the tool is frequently used with ball cutters where compensation is applied at the control.

First pass feed reduction

Reduces the feed rate of the first pass of the toolpath entering stock from the outside.

Maximum stepover

Enter the stepover as an absolute distance or as a percentage of the tool diameter.

NOTE

A smaller maximum stepover value may take longer to generate the toolpath.

Across

Cuts from one chain to the other, beginning at the start point of the first selected chain.

Along

Cuts in the along direction stepping over in the across direction.

Distance / Percent of stepover

Sets the spacing between the temporary across moves. These moves are used to generate final along cuts, but are not included in the final toolpath. This value can be entered here as an absolute value or as a percentage of the Maximum stepover.

Extend Entry/Exit

Use these fields to adjust the initial and final tool engagement with the material. Select the checkbox to active the option, then enter an Additional entry/exit distance. By default the initial cut is defined by the stepover amount. Mastercam adds the additional amount you specify to the beginning and/or end cut. These options are useful if the part geometry between the two chains is something other than a straight line, such as a slot that spans the corner of a part.

Stock to leave on walls

Sets the amount of stock on selected vertical machining geometry. Enter a positive value if you do not want to remove all stock or enter a negative value to remove more stock. If the machining geometry (or a portion of it) is not vertical, Mastercam interpolates a value between the Stock to leave on floors and Stock to leave on walls amounts.

Stock to leave on floors

Sets the amount of stock on selected horizontal machining geometry. Enter a positive value if you do not want to remove all stock or enter a negative value to remove more stock. If the machining geometry (or a portion of it) is not horizontal, Mastercam interpolates a value between the Stock to leave on floors and Stock to leave on walls amounts.
