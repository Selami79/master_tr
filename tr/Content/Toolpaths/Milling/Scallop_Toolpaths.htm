Scallop Toolpaths

The Surface Finish Scallop, 3D High Speed Scallop, and the 3D High Speed Equal Scallop toolpaths calculate the distance between offset passes in a 3D manner. These toolpaths measure the distance between offset passes along the topology of the model. Some toolpaths support optional input chains to influence the shape of the 3D offset passes. These toolpaths typically result in a uniform spacing of passes across the entire model.

The following image shows an Equal Scallop toolpath, cleaning a pocket.

Toolpath Guidelines

Before creating a Scallop toolpath, consider the following:

  • Scallop toolpaths are finishing toolpaths. In most cases, it is best practice to use them after roughing operations are complete.

  • 3D Scallop allows you to select machining geometry, but not avoidance geometry. Surface Finish Scallop and Equal Scallop allow you to select machining and avoidance geometry.

Types of Scallop operations

Mastercam offers three types of Scallop toolpaths: 3D HST Equal Scallop, 3D HST Scallop, and Surface Finish Scallop.

All three toolpaths create consistent scallop motion, maintaining the spacing between cuts to produce the motion. This type of toolpath is ideal to use on the boundaries generated by rest machining, or in any circumstances where you want to ensure a constant 3D distance between passes.

Equal Scallop and Scallop are 3D high speed toolpaths, which are machining strategies specially designed to produce the most efficient tool motion when machining surface models or solid faces.

The examples below have Rapid retract motion deselected in the Advanced Display function, to better show the cutting motion. Additionally, the red point indicates the selected Approximate start point for all three toolpaths.

Equal Scallop Scallop

Surface Finish Scallop is a surface finish/rough toolpath, typically designed to finish a part down to the drive geometry or the specified stock amount.

What are the differences between the toolpaths?

High Speed Equal Scallop and Scallop toolpaths are similar to Mastercam's standard Finish Scallop toolpath, but are enhanced to produce the smoother, free-flowing tool motion and transitions necessary for high speed machining.

Group

3D HST Scallop and Equal Scallop belong to the 3D high speed toolpaths and Surface Finish Scallop belongs to the surface finish/rough toolpaths. The toolpath group affects the parameters, geometry selection, and cutting speed. Learn more about these toolpath types in the 3D Mill and Router Toolpaths Help topic.

Location

Access 3D HST Scallop and Equal Scallop by clicking the Milling tab and selecting the operation from the 3D gallery. To access Surface Finish Scallop, right-click in the Toolpaths Manager and select Mill toolpaths, Surface Finish, Scallop.

Geometry Selection

Geometry selection for 3D HST Scallop and Equal Scallop is similar to the other 3D HST operations. After selecting the toolpath from the ribbon, the toolpath parameters dialog box opens. Use the Model Geometry and Toolpath Control pages in the toolpath dialog box to set machining regions and containment boundaries.

For Surface Finish Scallop operations, Mastercam prompts you to select drive geometry, check geometry, and a tool containment boundary. You can also select an approximate starting point. This process is similar to the geometry selection process for all surface finish/rough operations.

Parameters

The following parameters are unique to each type of Scallop operation:

  • The Surface Finish Scallop toolpath includes a unique option, Bias angle. The Bias angle refers to the angle at which Mastercam scans the part when creating the toolpath. The part is scanned in two directions, once at the bias angle and again at a right angle to it. For more information about the Bias angle, refer to the Bias Angle: Surface Finish Scallop Help topic.

  • Equal Scallop includes a Projected boundary smoothing option in the Toolpath Control page. This option attempts to improve the project boundary in cases where it is jagged or noisy when you are using Avoidance geometry and no containment boundary. The best practice is to create your toolpath, measure the amount of noise, and adjust this option as necessary.

  • Equal Scallop includes the Remove small segments option, which filters out any unwanted motion that is smaller than the entered value. This option is located in the Advanced page.

Similar to how Scallop operations share the geometry selection process of their toolpath group, Scallop operations also share parameters with other toolpaths in their group.

  • Equal Scallop operations include cut styles for Open contour direction and Closed contour directions. This is shared with Hybrid, Area Roughing, and Waterline 3D high speed toolpaths.

    Scallop includes different cut styles in the Cutting methods drop-down, similar to other 3D high speed toolpaths.

    Surface Finish Scallop does not have any type of cutting style option.

  • Equal Scallop and Scallop operations have advanced linking parameter options, to give you more control over leads, retracts, and transitions. You can access these options on the Linking Parameters page.

  • Surface Finish Scallop operations have Gap Settings that allow you to control what happens when a break in tool motion occurs in a Surface toolpath.

  • To control the minimum and maximum machining depth for the toolpath, most Surface Finish operations use Depth Limits. Similarly, most 3D HST toolpaths allow you to enter a maximum and minimum Z depth value on the Steep/Shallow page to establish the heights of the highest and lowest cutting passes. Learn more in Using Z Limits to Create a Machining Zone.

  • Surface Finish Scallop and Equal Scallop include an option that optimizes your machining to help reduce travel time and minimize tool retract. This option is Order cuts by minimum distance for Surface Finish Scallop and Optimize cut order for Equal Scallop.

What are the similarities between the toolpaths?

The following parameters are only used in the Scallop toolpath types:

  • All three toolpaths include the Expand inside to outside, which starts the toolpath in the center of the part and cuts towards the outside. The only other toolpath that includes this option is the Surface Finish Leftover toolpath.

  • Surface Finish Scallop and Equal Scallop include corner smoothing options. Corner smoothing for Surface Finish Scallop and Smoothing for Equal Scallop. This option smooths sharp corners and replaces them with curves. Eliminating sharp changes of direction results in a more even load on the tool and lets you consistently maintain a higher feed rate. Learn more in Corner Smoothing for Scallop Toolpaths.

  • Equal Scallop and Scallop include options to further modify how Mastercam drives the toolpath. You can choose between Closed offsets and Trimmed offsets. These options are affected by other options on the Toolpath Control and Steep/Shallow pages. You can learn more about these options in the Toolpath Control page Help topic.

  • Equal Scallop and Scallop toolpaths allow for the selection of curves. When curves are selected, the toolpath is created outward using the curves as a starting point. Curves can also influence the Closed offsets strategy. You can learn more about these options in the Toolpath Control page Help topic.

How do I decide between using a 3D high speed toolpath or a Surface toolpath?

When deciding between using a high speed toolpath or a Surface Rough or Finish toolpath, consider the following:

  • Toolpaths may have options and parameters that are unique to their group. For example, Surface toolpaths have more options for entering/exiting the part, and 3D high speed toolpaths have more options for linking parameters.

  • 3D HST toolpaths support holder collision checking when needed.

  • 3D HST toolpaths better support the use of stock models when needed.
